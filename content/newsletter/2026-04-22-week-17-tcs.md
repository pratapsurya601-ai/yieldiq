## Why I'm watching TCS this week

I keep coming back to a strange question: when did "predictable" become a slur in Indian markets?

Walk through any equity WhatsApp group right now and you'll hear breathless takes on the next AI darling, the next defence small-cap, the next quick-commerce IPO. What you will not hear, almost ever, is anybody making a serious case for TCS. The flagship of Indian IT services has become the stock people own but don't talk about. It pays its dividends, it does its buybacks, it grinds out 12-14% revenue growth, and the conversation has moved on.

That's exactly when I get interested.

Here is what TCS actually looks like today, by the YieldIQ numbers:

- **ROCE of 54.9%.** Not a typo. The company puts a rupee of capital in and gets 55 paise of operating profit out, every year, year after year. There are maybe ten businesses in India that clear this bar. TCS has been one of them for over a decade.
- **Piotroski 7/9.** A clean balance sheet, improving margins, no funny business with working capital. The kind of score that quietly accumulates when a business is healthy and management isn't doing anything stupid.
- **Margin of safety: 32.7%.** Our DCF — even on conservative assumptions about AI cannibalisation of legacy services revenue — pegs fair value meaningfully above where the market has it.
- **Wide moat in IT services.** Switching costs in enterprise IT outsourcing are brutal. Once a Fortune 500 client embeds you into their core systems, ripping you out costs them three years and a small fortune. TCS has been embedded for two decades.

## What YieldIQ sees

The market's narrative right now is roughly: AI eats services, services revenue compresses, the big four (TCS / Infy / Wipro / HCL) face structural margin pressure, multiples should compress.

That narrative is not wrong. It is just early. And it is being applied uniformly to companies that have very different exposures to it.

TCS earns a meaningful share of its revenue from BFSI and retail digital transformation work that AI augments rather than replaces — at least on a 3-5 year view. The company has been quietly hiring AI-fluent engineers, restructuring billing models towards outcome-based pricing, and pushing its TCS Bancs and ignio platforms harder. None of this guarantees the old growth rate. But it tells you the company is not standing still while the music changes.

Meanwhile the multiple compression has already happened. TCS today trades at a P/E roughly 25% below its five-year average, against a backdrop where it is generating record free cash flow and returning almost all of it to shareholders.

## Bottom line

TCS is the kind of position you build slowly, while everyone else is busy chasing the next thing — and the kind that quietly compounds while you stop checking the price.
